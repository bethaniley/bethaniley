# Education

- Thesis title: “Conspiracy theory beliefs and dis/misinformation about gender equality, diversity and inclusion initiatives: An intergroup perspective.” [Open access](https://pure.qub.ac.uk/en/studentTheses/conspiracy-theory-beliefs-and-dismisinformation-about-gender-equa/).

## Political Psychology MSc
University of Kent (Canterbury, UK). Pass with Distinction. September 2019 - July 2021.
- Dissertation title: “System threat and political identification as predictors of bipartisan climate change conspiracy theories: A pre-registered study of British adults.” Supervised by Professor Karen Douglas. This dissertation was conducted using open science practices and was graded at distinction (75%).
- Modules included: Political Psychology; Advanced Topics in Intergroup Relations; Current Issues in Social and Applied Psychology II: Applications; and Public Opinion: Nature and Measurement.

First Class Honours.
September 2015 – July 2019.
